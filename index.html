The Opportunity

Unrealized Potential

60 Years of Waiting

The National Park Service has managed the Sutro Baths site as a "stabilized ruin" for six decades, with no plan for restoration or activation.

Underinvested

Infrastructure needs and deferred maintenance have accumulated, while the site's potential to serve the Bay Area community remains untapped.

Inaccessible to All

The current site lacks ADA accessibility, excluding countless visitors from experiencing this historic treasure.

The Legacy

The Sutro Baths: A San Francisco Icon

Built in 1896 by Adolph Sutro, the Sutro Baths were once the world's largest indoor swimming establishment. The magnificent glass-enclosed structure housed seven swimming pools of varying temperatures, accommodating 10,000 visitors at a time.

Our Vision

A New Chapter for Sutro

Through a Section 111 Historic Lease, we propose a sustainable restoration that balances private investment with public benefit.

Zone A

Private Sanctuary

A world-class luxury thermal spa and wellness facility, drawing on the site's historic identity as a bathing destination.

  • Ocean-view thermal pools
  • Premium wellness treatments
  • Sustainable revenue generation
  • Historic architecture reimagined
Zone B

Public Access

Free or nominal-cost public amenities, subsidized by Zone A revenue, ensuring the site remains accessible to all.

  • Ocean-fed tidal pools
  • Observation decks
  • Interpretive center
  • Full ADA accessibility

Reimagined for Tomorrow

The Path Forward

Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1

Federal Engagement

Months 1-6

Federal lobbying to secure a Request for Interest from the National Park Service.

Phase 2

Lease & Capital

Months 6-18

Securing the historic lease agreement and raising pre-development capital.

Phase 3

Regulatory Approval

Months 18-36

Navigating California Coastal Commission review and environmental compliance.

Phase 4

Construction

Month 36+

Groundbreaking and construction of the restored Sutro Baths facility.
